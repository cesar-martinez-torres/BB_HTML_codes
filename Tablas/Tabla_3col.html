Unit Description
Content
1. General concepts The student will understand the basic components and structure of a robotic arm and explain how position and orientation are represented in robotics, including the use of Cartesian coordinates and Euler angles. 1.1 Introduction
1.2 Parts of a robotic arm
1.3 Representing position and orientation
2. Robot arm kinematics Upon completion of this unit, students will be able to understand the basics of robotics kinematics, including the principles of direct and inverse kinematics and use kinematic analysis to design and plan the motion of a robotic manipulator for a given task. 2.1 Introduction
2.2 Forward kinematics
2.3 Inverse kinematics
3. Differential kinematics Upon completion of this unit, students will be able to understand the basics of robot arm dynamics and its role in robotics, obtain the dynamic model of a robot manipulator and use it to design and plan the motion for a given task, taking into account the forces and torques required to perform it. 3.1 Introduction
3.2 Geometric Jacobian
3.3 Analytical Jacobian
4. Robot arm dynamics Upon completion of this unit, students will be able to understand the basics of robot arm dynamics and its role in robotics, obtain the dynamic model of a robot manipulator and use it to design and plan the motion for a given task, taking into account the forces and torques required to perform it. 4.1 Introduction
4.2 Newton-Euler formulation
4.3 Dynamic model of manipulators
5. Planning and control Upon completion of this unit, students will be able to use different techniques to generate smooth, feasible trajectories for a robotic manipulator and implement ontrol algorithms to execute such trajectory and achieve a desired task. 5.1 Introduction
5.2 Trajectory generation
5.3 Joint space control
5.4 Operational space control
